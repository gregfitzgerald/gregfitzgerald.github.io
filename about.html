About

I'm a neuroscience researcher with 15 years of experience spanning lab management, experimental research, and quantitative analysis. I currently work in the Behavioral Neuroscience Lab at the University at Albany, where my doctoral research uses meta-analysis to evaluate Alzheimer's disease interventions — work that uncovered a 2.5× gap between how well treatments work in rodents versus humans.

Before Albany, I managed a laboratory at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, where I gained hands-on experience running research operations, managing animal colonies, and coordinating with multiple research teams. That operational experience informs how I approach research today: systematically, with attention to both the science and the logistics that make good science possible.

What I Do

My work sits at the intersection of neuroscience and research methodology. I use quantitative tools — meta-analysis, systematic review, statistical modeling — to synthesize large bodies of preclinical research and identify patterns that individual studies can't reveal. My key finding so far: rodent models of AD consistently overestimate treatment effects by approximately 2.5×, which has direct implications for how the pharmaceutical industry evaluates drug candidates in their pipeline.

I've published in Cell (Impact Factor 64.5), managed a 500+ mouse breeding colony, taught statistics and research methods for 7 semesters, and built web applications as portfolio projects. I bring a rare combination of deep scientific expertise and practical technical skills.

Research Interests

  • Neurotrophic factors and aging: How growth factors like IGF-2 influence brain health and cognitive function across the lifespan
  • Meta-analysis methodology: Systematic review techniques for synthesizing neuroscience and psychology research
  • Translational research: Bridging the gap between preclinical findings and clinical outcomes in Alzheimer's disease
  • Research reproducibility: Improving statistical practices, study design, and transparency in behavioral research
